Koenigsberger Klopse

Ingredients

  • 1 Roll (from the previous day)
  • 2 shallots
  • 1 tablespoon butter and 1 organic egg
  • 3 stalks of parsley (smooth)
  • 600g of veal mince (preferably organic)
  • salt and pepper (fresh)
  • 1½ liters of beef broth
  • 30 tablespoon butter
  • 40 g flour
  • 100 ml of white wine (or broth)
  • 150 g of whipped cream
  • 2 organic egg yolk
  • 60 g capers (small; drained)

Description

1. Soak the rolls. Peel and chop shallots and sauté in butter until translucent. Rinse anchovies and parsley and chop finely. Squeeze rolls and finely chop. Knead rolls, shallots, anchovies, parsley, minced meat, egg, salt and pepper well. With hands well moistened, roll into about 16 meatballs.

2. Bring stock to a boil in a large shallow saucepan. Cook meatballs over low heat for 10-12 minutes. Remove with a skimmer and keep warm.

3. Melt the butter and brown the flour in it. Measure out 375 ml of the cooking broth from the meatballs. Slowly pour the broth and wine into the pot while stirring. Bring sauce to a boil while stirring, simmer for about 10-15 minutes.

4. Whisk cream and egg yolk and add some of the hot sauce. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir the egg cream into the remaining hot sauce. Heat again, stirring, until just before boiling point. Stir in capers, season with salt, pepper and lemon juice. Slowly heat the meatballs in the sauce, but do not let them boil.
